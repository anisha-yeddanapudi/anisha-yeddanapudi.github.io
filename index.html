About Me

I'm currently a pursuing a PhD under Prof. Lee Cronin at the University of Glasgow. Prior to joining the Cronin Group, I recieved my BA in Physics and Applied Math at the University of California, Berkeley with Honors. My current research focuses on understanding how complex inorganic systems arise from their components. I enjoy discovering and understanding new physics, and as a result I've worked on a wide variety of projects ranging from condensed matter to particle physics.

  • Topological Analysis of Energy Reconstruction in CUORE Calorimeters
    This work aims to understand and correct biases in the calorimetric energy reconstruction by classifying these errors in terms of event topology, location, and possible variation of the pulse shape. Overall, we find evidence of position-dependent energy reconstruction in CUORE's TeO2 calorimeters.
